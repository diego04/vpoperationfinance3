Samer Sleiman

Vice President Operations & Finance

 

Hey! My name is Samer Sleiman and I am running to be your Vice President of Operations and Finance. I thank you for taking the time to look at my website, but first let me tell you a little bit about myself...

 

...A Little Bit About Myself

I am nearing completion of my B.A. in Honors Economics, at the University of Alberta. In the summer of 2011, I started my own business that I managed until the end of 2014 while juggling a full course load. Demonstrating my ability to handle multiple tasks, I always complete them with the same integrity I began with. Having previous experience with accounting and financials of businesses, I believe that this has refined my skills to excel in this position. Along with spending the last year on Students’ Council, followed by my active involvement in many student groups including the Muslim Students Association and Sponsoring Orphans Worldwide; I have had the privilege to work with the diverse communities that the University of Alberta has to offer.

As a participating councilor on the Student’s Union and member of numerous student groups, I see the potential for growth to enhance student’s experiences. The VP Operations & Finance is responsible for overseeing the Students’ Union’s $10.37 million budget along with the daily operations that follow. The primary focus of my platform is to address the North Power Plant redevelopment, provide diverse options for food on campus, and to improve communication between the Students’ Union and the student body. Every stakeholder should be held accountable, be it the VP OF or the University administrator, as we all share a common goal: to provide a better experience for all students on campus.

Why I am Interested

Vice President Operations & Finance appeals to me based on my experiences as a councilor. Having the opportunity to view the roles of each executive along with their responsibilities and portfolios, I have the skills to be VP Operations and Finance. First and foremost as a student, I understand the struggles students face including (but not limited to) wanting a variety of food options to having a clear understanding of where our money is going. These are concerns I provide not only recommendations in, but also a solution.

My Promises

 

Week of Welcome in the Winter semester

Week of Welcome is one of the union’s most important events hosted; it engages students in campus life whilst furthering students. As student’s interests develop throughout the school year, the possibility to join student groups will improve membership recruitment while creating an environment to help tackle mental health issues. I envision Winter clubs fare to be held in the Butterdome. Working alongside with the Executive Committee I will be able to grant approval for such events to take place.

Transparency Reports

Where is your money going? Good question. Student’s pay a mandatory fee of $105.08 per semester to the Student’s Union, $9 per semester towards SUB renovations and $26 per semester towards the PAW fund, to subsidize the cost of the new center. It is imperative the SU develops a transparent relationship with students in regards to the collection and allocation of these fees. Student’s money must be accounted which is why quarterly reports must be sent out to ensure this.

Remodel Dewey’s

The SU must revitalize Dewey’s making it the “it spot” it once claimed. It is my duty to utilize campus space to maximize profits; and with this lounge being at the heart of the University, why not attain this objective as displayed? As demonstrated in the Financials of Dewey’s Graph (see figure 1 on page 4), Dewey’s net profits have not been consistent throughout the years. It is essential to stabilize this issue. It is something as simple as improving the lighting to create an inviting mood. This ultimately brings in sustainable profit while creating a relaxing space and sense of community.

Increasing Accessibility of Bylaws Through Languages

Having a multilingual student body, communication is a necessity. In order for the SU to develop a relationship with their students, it is vital to create a sense of inclusion. An initial step is to make our bylaws accessible for French-speaking students. To make this feasible I am taking the initiative to work with AUFSJ to translate the bylaws into French. This has the potential to be carried out throughout the years, hopefully to expand into the translation into multiple languages.

SUTV

TV’s located in SUB, Cameron, Lister and CCIS are great resources to deploy information throughout all areas on campus. These TV’s provide opportunities to promote student pride whether it be athletic highlights to campus and student group events. Installing a live newsfeed linked to The Gateway will serve as an interactive tool to ensure all students remain informed of activities occurring on campus. This is also an opportunity to give acknowledgment to hard-working writers and journalists whose deserves to be recognized.

Nutritional Information and Diversified Food Options

Having a variety of food options available is key in regaining student’s popularity within SU food establishments. This includes gluten-free, halal and kosher options along with greater accessibility in healthier choices. Menus that provide information on nutritional content are a step forward towards a healthier campus.
